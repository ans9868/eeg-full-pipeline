## Key Findings

1. **Single-fold performance can vary dramatically**: The most variable combination (SVM + ANOVA, L_2) shows a range of **75.7 percentage points** (16.9% to 92.6%).

2. **ANOVA features show higher variance**: ANOVA-based configurations generally show larger fold-to-fold variance compared to PCA-based configurations, especially in L_2 (leave-2-out) scenarios.

3. **L_2 shows more variance than L_6**: Leave-2-out configurations consistently show larger ranges than Leave-6-out configurations, as expected with smaller test sets.

4. **Median + IQR required**: These examples clearly demonstrate why reporting median and IQR (interquartile range) over many folds is essential for robust evaluation. A single fold can be highly misleading.

5. **Model-dependent variance**: Different models show different variance patterns:
   - **SVM + ANOVA (L_2)**: Highest variance (75.7% span)
   - **MLP + ANOVA (L_2)**: High variance but better minimum (62.2% span, min 36.1%)
   - **KNN + ANOVA (L_2)**: Lower variance but still significant (47.4% span)

---

## Recommendations

1. **Always report median + IQR** across all folds, not just mean ± std
2. **Avoid single-fold evaluations** - they can be highly misleading
3. **Consider fold-level variance** when interpreting results
4. **Use random fold generation** (50+ folds) for better variance estimation
5. **Report both min and max** to show the full range of possible outcomes
