## Key Design Decisions

### LPSO (Leave-P-Subjects-Out)

- **P = 6**: Primary setting. Each fold holds out 6 subjects (balanced: 3 Alzheimer's + 3 Control). Used for all main performance claims.
- **P = 2**: Variance stress test. Each fold holds out 2 subjects. Wider IQR and more extreme fold-to-fold swings are expected by design.

### Fold Generation Strategies

- **Random-50**: 50 folds generated by random sampling of P subjects from the available pool. No constraint that every subject must appear in at least one test fold.
- **Systematic-12**: 12 folds generated deterministically with balanced subject distribution. Every subject appears in at least one test fold.

### Subject Coverage Note (P=2)

The P=2 experiments cover only **49 of 65 subjects** (not all 65). This is a natural consequence of random sampling without replacement constraints:

- For any single subject, the probability of never being selected across 50 P=2 folds is approximately (63/65)^50 ~ 21%.
- The expected number of "never-tested" subjects is 65 x 0.21 ~ 14, which matches the observed 16 missing subjects (65 - 49).
- This does not affect the statistical validity of the P=2 results within their 49-subject pool; it means P=6 vs P=2 comparisons are on slightly different subject populations.

### Within-Subject Experiments

- Single 80/20 train/test split where the **same subjects** appear in both sets.
- 3 hyperparameter configurations per model (not folds).
- Included as an optimistic performance ceiling / contrast, not as the primary evaluation.

### Models (all experiments)

Four models, each with 3 hyperparameter configurations per fold/split:

- **KNN** (K-Nearest Neighbors)
- **SVM** (Support Vector Machine)
- **XGBoost** (Gradient Boosted Trees)
- **MLP** (Multi-Layer Perceptron / Neural Network)
