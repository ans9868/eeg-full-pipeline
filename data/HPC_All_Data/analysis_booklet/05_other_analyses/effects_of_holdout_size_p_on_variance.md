# 6. Effects of Hold-out Size (P) on Variance

## Why this matters

In LPSO, the hold-out size **P** controls how "cohort-like" each fold is. Smaller P (e.g., P = 2) makes each fold more idiosyncratic, leading to larger fold-to-fold variance. Balanced P = 6 (3 ALZ + 3 CNTRL) stabilizes medians much better.

## Protocol

Compare 50-fold LPSO (Random) at **P = 6** vs **P = 2** under identical settings.

## Result

Medians are similar, but IQR and min/max widen at P = 2. Variance inflation at P = 2 is consistent across all models (KNN, SVM, XGBoost, MLP) and both feature sets (PCA and ANOVA), so it is not a single-model or single-feature artifact.

**Reporting choice:** P = 6 is the primary estimate; P = 2 is a variance stress test.
